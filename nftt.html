NFT's

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token. It's a digital certificate of ownership, stored on a blockchain, that proves the uniqueness and authenticity of a specific digital item, like art, music, or collectibles. Unlike cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin or Ethereum, NFTs are not interchangeable with one another. Each NFT has a distinct value and cannot be exchanged on a one-to-one basis. They've gained popularity in the art and entertainment industries, allowing creators to sell and collectors to own unique digital assets. NFT ownership is recorded on a decentralized ledger, providing proof of authenticity and provenance. However, owning an NFT doesn't always grant copyright or reproduction rights, which can be a source of confusion. The NFT market has seen significant growth, but it's still a relatively new and evolving space.
